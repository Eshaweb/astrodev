Indian Astrology("Vedanga Jyotisha")

Vedanga Jyotisha is one of the earliest known Indian texts on astronomy or astrology. ‘Jyotisha’ is one among the six entities of Vedas or is termed as Vedanga, along with ‘Shiksha’, ‘Vyakarana’, ‘Chanda’, ‘Nirukta’ and ‘Kalpa’. Soorya, Brahma, Vyasa and 18 other eminent personalities of the forgone era have been the proponents of ‘Jyotisha’. It comprises of six parts, namely the ‘Jataka’, ‘Gola’, ‘Nimitta’, ‘Prashna’, ‘Muhurtha’ and ‘Ganita’. Among these, ‘Jataka’ furnishes the benefice or malefic results based on the planetary positions, ‘Gola’ renders information with respect to the celestial bodies or astronomical objects, ‘Nimitta’ provides an insight to omens and its consequences, ‘Muhurtha’ signifies the auspiciousness of a particular time for any ritual, ‘Prashna’ helps to find solutions to various apprehensions or doubts whereas ‘Ganita’ helps us analyse the strength of the planets.

According to the ancient beliefs or Shastras, every living being experiences a life, which may be good or bad, based on one’s own ‘Karma’. But, one can know the malefic or benefice aspects of one’s life due to the influence of the past lives from the planetary positions at the time of his or her birth, as well as the effects after the birth, could be predicted as per the ‘Karma’ from the Prashna Shastra. It is also believed that one would experience the best and the worst during the given life itself. Hence, Jyotisha Shastra is a medium to know about such factors quite exhaustively. But, as a word of caution one has to keep in mind that nothing in this world is foolproof and we too hereby clearly state that, as any other discipline like medicine or law, astrology too, might have its own limitations.

Ashtamangala

Ashtamangala prashne is a form of practice under Vedic astrology, followed widely in Kerala, where usually a Navagraha pooja is performed to seek blessings from the Navagraha. After the pooja, with the arrangement of cowry shells, sandalwood, floral offerings, gold coin etc are placed in the hands of a little child. On the basis of the placement of the gold coin randomly in one of the twelve houses drawn in front of the astrologer, a review or diagnosis is done, which in turn takes some other aspects or omens also into consideration, like a lamp, Tamboola etc for a proper assessment. Shraddha Maasika

Shraddha is a ritual performed by the heirs in honour of the dead on the day of death as per the lunar or the solar calendar. It is obligatory on part of the successor to perform such rite every year since it implies his dedication towards his elders or ancestors. According to the Smrithi Granthas, it is believed that if anyone abstains from performing such duties, he or she may suffer in his or her life with issues related to their progeny, epilepsy, mental illness and so on, which could adversely affect the quality of their life as well. Hence, Shraddha is believed to be a very important ritual or to say Karma to be performed in the lifetime of an individual.
